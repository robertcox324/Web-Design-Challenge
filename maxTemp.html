Max Temperature

The plot on this page displays the correlation of a city's latitude to the Maximum temperature it experienced on the particular date the data was gathered. As can be expected, cities closer to the equater experienced higher temperatures than those further away and closer to the poles. Additionally, the southern hemisphere cities represented in this data tended to experience higher temperatures than those in the northern hemisphere at comparable distances from the equater at the time of year this data was gathered. This makes sense, as summer and winter in the southern hemisphere occur during opposite times of the year from the northern hemisphere, so the northern hemisphere winter-spring season this was observed in would be the end of summer for the southern hemisphere
